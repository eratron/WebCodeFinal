Hot Rolled

Hot rolled steel is a versatile and widely used form of steel produced by rolling heated steel ingots or blooms through a series of high-pressure rollers at temperatures exceeding 1,700°F (927°C). This intense heat allows the steel to be shaped and formed with minimal resistance,ensuring good quality

Cold Rolled

Cold rolled steel, the refined counterpart to hot rolled steel, undergoes a distinct manufacturing process to achieve superior dimensional accuracy, surface finish, and mechanical properties. Unlike hot rolled steel, which is shaped at high temperatures, cold rolled steel is passed through a series of rollers at room temperature.
